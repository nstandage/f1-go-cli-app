Why Your Goroutine is Hanging

A visual guide to channels, goroutines, and deadlocks in Go

The Two Key Concepts

🔀 Goroutines
A goroutine is a lightweight thread that runs concurrently alongside your main program. You launch one with go someFunction(). The key thing: launching a goroutine doesn't pause your current code — both run at the same time. But if your main goroutine gets blocked before launching it, it never starts.
📡 Unbuffered Channels
An unbuffered channel (make(chan *Event)) is a synchronous hand-off. A sender (c <- value) will block and wait until a receiver is ready. A receiver (<-c) will block and wait until a sender is ready. Both sides must be ready simultaneously — like a relay baton pass.
💀 Deadlock
A deadlock happens when a goroutine is waiting for something that will never happen. In your case: main is waiting for data on a channel, but nothing will ever send to that channel because the goroutine that would do the sending hasn't been started yet.

🎾 The Real-World Analogy
An unbuffered channel is like a tennis ball machine and a player. The goroutine (ball machine) fires balls into the channel. The main goroutine (player) stands at the other end ready to hit them.

Your current code makes the player show up and get into ready stance first — but then never turns the machine on. The player just stands there forever. The fix: turn the machine on first, then have the player step up to receive.
